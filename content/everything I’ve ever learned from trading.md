A collection of aphorisms about trading that I’ve learned over the years. 

--- 
You want to buy something that’s going to move, not just because the vol is low.

---
Things can be high for a reason, and often are. Be wary of selling them.

--- 
You should be thinking in distributions. When your distribution differs the market’s, that’s where you find edge.

--- 
There are many ways to make money in the market. Find out which way works for you.
- Find the style of trading that you are good at and vibes with your soul. 
- There are a million ways to make a living in markets. Whether that’s HFT, long/short equity, market making, vol arb, swing trading, event trading, momentum/trend following, shitco trading, dispersion, stat arb, whatever it is. Just find one you enjoy and get really, really good at it. 
--- 
Contrary to popular belief, both parties in a trade can win. For example, if someone is buying a naked call option because they think the underlying will go up. And I think that the volatility on that option is too high and will go down, then I can sell them that call option, hedge my delta risk, and then profit if volatility goes down, and then they can win on the call option if the stock goes up.

--- 
You don’t have to be right most of the time, as long as when you are right, you’re really right and you win in a big way.
- Most of the money is made on a small amount of trades that had big payouts

--- 
Trading is a marathon. Don’t expect the money to be fast and consistent. Think in terms of a career. I’m talking years.
- When I was young and stupid, I thought I’d be able to make a bunch of money really fast. Right out of college, I joined a prop firm that focused on day trading equities. I struggled immensely with the strategy, and after a little over a year I called it quits. I was disillusioned. I gave up trading. Several years later, I found myself drawn again to markets, this time through the lens of a different strategy. It seemed much more my speed, I intuited the strategy much more, and over time I realized that this job takes a long time to get good at. Markets can be extremely frustrating at times, and at other times very rewarding. If you keep trying, and don’t give up, there will end up being a market that you can do really well in, and actually make life-changing sums of money. It’s 100% not a get-rich-quick scheme, but more like a get-rich-over-time-and-if-you-try-really-hard-and-also-get-lucky scheme. So, just keep going, and when you do experience good market times, hold that memory tight and remember it when you’re knees deep in bad trading markets. - from someone old and stupid.

--- 
Uncertainty is where the money can be made. It’s all about expectations. What does the market expect to happen versus what do you expect might happen. 

--- 
True, mechanical arbitrages are exceedingly rare. If you think you see a mispricing somewhere, ask yourself why does this exist, and is it actually exploitable? 
- Often mispricings can be due to illiquidity, transaction costs, or other real world things that would actually prevent you from making a profit on it if you were to try to close the arb.

--- 
With regard to intraday delta hedging, you should only hedge if you believe that you have directional alpha in that moment.
