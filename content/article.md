An article is a word used before a noun to show whether the noun is specific or general. There are two types: **definite article**, refers to something specific, and **indefinite article**, which refers to something non-specific.

Examples:
**Definite article**: “I read **the** book you recommended.
**Indefinite articles:** “I saw **a** dog in the park.” “She ate **an** apple.”
