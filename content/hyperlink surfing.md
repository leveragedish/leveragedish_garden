Hyperlink surfing is when you traverse the internet mostly by clicking through different links. Its most evident when you're on Wikipedia looking something up, and then an hour later you're on a completely different side of the internet. I love it so much. Its very freeing to just be able to explore without being explicitly led in any particular direction. Its like following the [[desire path]] of the internet.

People have even made a game around link surfing called [Wiki-Race](https://wiki-race.com/), where you can play against your friends to see who can have the fastest time getting from point A to point B on Wikipedia. 

Surfing the internet in this way is refreshing, you get to avoid the optimization algorithms that constantly feed you "what they think you ought to see", and actually get to see exactly what you want to see. It's a unique experience that you can only really get by being on the real internet, website to website. If you go on social media it simply can't be done. It's a pure remnant of the original internet, just infinite paths reaching out into the unknown, and each path you pick through the web of hyperlinks is a unique experience.
